A CDN works by caching your website's static content in servers located strategically around the world. When a user requests your website, the CDN delivers the content from the server closest to the user, reducing latency and improving load times.

Using a CDN offers several benefits, including improved website performance, faster page load times, reduced bandwidth costs, enhanced scalability, global reach, and better user experiences.

A CDN can have a positive impact on SEO. Faster page load times and improved website performance contribute to better user experience, which search engines consider when ranking websites. Additionally, CDNs can help with global reach, reducing latency, and improving website accessibility.

Yes, CDNs play a crucial role in mobile optimization. With the increasing number of mobile users, CDNs deliver content efficiently to mobile devices, reducing load times, conserving data usage, and ensuring a smooth browsing experience.

Yes, CDNs enhance website reliability and uptime. By distributing your content across multiple servers, CDNs provide redundancy, load balancing, and failover mechanisms, ensuring that your website remains accessible even during peak traffic or server failures.
